Consuelo Palacio, M.B.A, Ph.D

Consuelo Palacio is a distinguished academic professional with a Graduate degree in Education Administration and minor in Curriculum Instruction from the esteemed University of Wisconsin – Milwaukee. Her academic journey culminated in the completion of a groundbreaking dissertation titled “The Career Development of Latina Women Achieving the Position of Public High School Principal.” This qualitative study, framed through the Social Cognitive Career Theory, delved into the underrepresentation of Latina women in high school principal roles and explored the unique experiences that shape their career trajectories.

With a keen focus on promoting diversity and inclusion in educational leadership, Consuelo’s research sheds light on the challenges and opportunities faced by Latina women aspiring to leadership positions in public education. Her commitment to empowering Latina professionals is further demonstrated through her ongoing book project aimed at advocating for greater representation of Latina women as high school principals in comprehensive high schools.

In addition to her academic pursuits, Consuelo has a diverse professional background that encompasses roles in the mortgage industry, tax preparation management, substitute teaching, graduate research, and administrative assistance in the field of education. Her experience as a graduate research student and committee member on a pivotal study at the University of Wisconsin – Milwaukee underscores her dedication to fostering inclusive environments for faculty and staff from diverse backgrounds.

As a former student advisor and administrative assistant in the Department of Education, Consuelo played a pivotal role in supporting college students pursuing degrees in teacher education and supervising educational resources. Her expertise in curriculum design, employee supervision, and customer service management reflects her versatile skill set and commitment to excellence in every endeavor.

Consuelo Palacio’s multifaceted background, academic achievements, and research contributions make her a valuable asset to the academic community. Her passion for advancing the representation and success of Latina women in educational leadership positions continues to drive her impactful work in the field of education.
