Study: Teacher to High School Principal

Abstract

This qualitative study examines the trajectory of Latina women ascending to public high school principal roles, utilizing the Social Cognitive Career Theory framework. Despite facing significant underrepresentation, particularly in states lacking any representation, the path to principalship remains unclear for few Latina women. Understanding the factors driving their representation is crucial, especially considering their sparse presence, primarily in states with sizable Latino student populations. With the U.S. Census projecting continuous growth in Latino student enrollment, the evolving demographics of public schools across the United States are evident. This inquiry centers on how Latina women articulate their experiences navigating career paths toward high school principalship. What guidance do Latina principals offer women aspiring the role of high school administrator?

In this course, we'll heavily lean on primary texts, interpreting the journey to high school principalship through the firsthand accounts of Latina women. We'll explore six key focal points:


  • Constructing the landscape and context for the dissertation study
  • The early familia influence: woman's role; motivations and Decision-making; reflecting on familial influences
  • Corporate America's impact on evaluation: Program and/or intervention on the target population; Outcome-focused evaluation in educational settings
  • Transitioning from Corporate America to Teaching: Teachers integrate corporate business skills into educational roles
  • The career trajectory to the Administration role: Advancing to administrative; Identificaton of common pathways, challenges, and srategies for advancement
  • The high school principal skill sets: Accessing soft skills, technical competencies, and data management skills required for high school principals
  • Key findings, implications for educational leadership practice and policy; Recommendations supporting women's career trajectories in educational ladership beyond the principalship

Consuelo Palacio

Consuelo Palacio is a distinguished academic professional with a Graduate degree in Education Administration and minor in Curriculum Instruction from the esteemed University of Wisconsin – Milwaukee. Her academic journey culminated in the completion of a groundbreaking dissertation titled “The Career Development of Latina Women Achieving the Position of Public High School Principal.”


She is the author of numerous books, including: Living in Two Worlds: The Causes of the Inner Perplexities, Marianisma (for which she received the Notoriety Prize), and Maldon: A Memoir. She is also a frequent contributor to The LinkedIn News Hour & The Education Simple Online Learning Website and the Education Simple YouTube Channel.
